Conference on Reproducibility and Replicability in Economics and the Social Sciences (CRRESS) starts on September 27, 2022.


The Conference on Reproducibility and Replicability in Economics and the Social Sciences (CRRESS) is a monthly series of virtual and in-person conferences on the topics of reproducibility, replicability, and transparency in the social sciences, starting on September 27, 2022. Each month, a panel of experts will discuss a particular topic with relevance to the overall theme of reproducibility and transparency. We invite anyone with an interest in social science research to attend.

The first panel, on September 27, 2022, will address the question “Should journals verify reproducibility”, We are delighted to welcome Guido Imbens (Editor of Econometrica), Tim Salmon (Economic Inquiry), and Toni Whited (Journal of Financial Economics). Please register to join us on Zoom.

The purpose of scientific publishing is the dissemination of robust research findings, exposing them to the scrutiny of peers and other interested parties. Scientific articles should accurately and completely provide information on the origin and provenance of data and on the analytical and computational methods used. Yet in recent years, doubts about the adequacy of the information provided in scientific articles and their addenda have been voiced.

The various sessions will address numerous topics: the initiation of research, the conduct of research, the preparation of research for publication, and the scrutiny after publication. Hosted on Zoom or in-person at sessions organized at leading conferences, we welcome all interested to attend the whole series!

Undergraduates, graduate students, and career researchers will be able to learn about best practices for transparent, reproducible, and scientifically sound research in the social sciences.

CRRESS is organized by Lars Vilhuber and Aleksandr Michuda, Cornell University, and funded by the National Science Foundation. Participation is free, but registration is required. Most sessions will be recorded and made available on our website. An online book, collecting short articles by each of our panelists related to their presentation, will be made available progressively over the course of the year. All information on registration, recordings, and articles, can be found on our website at https://labordynamicsinstitute.github.io/crress/.
